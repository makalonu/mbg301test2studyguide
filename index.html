MGB 301: Test 2 Study Guide (Spring 2026)

CH 9: GROUP BEHAVIOR

Group: 2+ individuals, interacting/interdependent, achieving specific objectives.

Why Join? Security, status, self-esteem, affiliation, power, goal achievement.

5-Stage Model

  • Forming: Uncertainty about purpose/leaders.
  • Storming: Intragroup conflict; resisting control.
  • Norming: Cohesiveness, identity, standard behavior forms.
  • Performing: Fully functional, energy on task.
  • Adjourning: Wrap-up (for temp groups).

Group Properties

1. Roles: Expected behavior patterns.

  • Identity: Attitudes/behaviors consistent with role.
  • Perception: Our view of how we should act.
  • Expectations: Others' view of how we should act.
  • Psych Contract: Unwritten expectations between employer & employee.
  • Role Conflict: Divergent expectations (e.g., family vs work).

2. Norms: Acceptable standards of behavior.

  • Hawthorne Studies: Worker behavior highly influenced by group norms & social identity, not just money/environment.
  • Conformity (Asch): Adjusting behavior to align with group; fear of standing out.
  • Negative Norms: Deviant workplace behavior (production, property, political, aggression).

3. Status: Socially defined rank. Status Characteristics Theory says it derives from: 1) Power a person wields over others, 2) Ability to contribute to goals, 3) Personal characteristics. High status members can deviate from norms more easily.

4. Size: Social Loafing: Tendency to expend less effort in a group (Ringelmann effect). Prevent by: setting group goals, peer evaluation, individual accountability.

5. Cohesiveness: Attraction to group & motivation to stay.
High Coh + High Perf Norms = Highest Productivity.
High Coh + Low Perf Norms = Low Productivity.

Decision Making

Strengths: More info, diversity of views, higher quality, acceptance of solution.

Weaknesses: Time consuming, conformity pressure, dominated by few, ambiguous responsibility.

  • Groupthink: Consensus norm overrides realistic appraisal of alternatives. Deters minority views.
  • Groupshift: Group discussions lead to more extreme positions (often riskier) than individual initial views.
  • Brainstorming: Idea generation, strictly no criticism allowed.
  • NGT (Nominal Group Tech): Silent idea generation -> round-robin presentation -> discussion -> independent secret ranking. (Outperforms brainstorming).

CH 10: WORK TEAMS

Groups vs Teams: Groups share info/make decisions (Neutral/Neg Synergy). Teams work toward specific goal with joint effort (Positive Synergy - whole is > sum of parts).

Process Loss: When team performance is worse than individual potential due to coordination/motivation issues.

Types of Teams

  • Problem-Solving: 5-12 people, same dept, meet a few hours/week to improve quality/efficiency. Cannot implement changes.
  • Self-Managed: 10-15 people, take on former supervisor responsibilities (planning, scheduling, acting on problems).
  • Cross-Functional: Employees from same hierarchical level but different work areas (Task forces, committees).
  • Virtual: Dispersed members linked via tech. Lack paraverbal/nonverbal cues; need high trust.

Team Effectiveness Model

  • Context: Adequate resources, Leadership/Structure, Climate of trust, Performance eval & reward systems.
  • Composition: Abilities of members, Personality (Conscientiousness/Openness), Allocating roles, Diversity, Size (ideal 5-9), Member preferences.
  • Work Design: Autonomy, Skill variety, Task identity, Task significance (enhances motivation).
  • Process: Common purpose, Specific goals (SMART), Team efficacy (belief in success), Conflict levels (task conflict can be good), Social loafing minimization.

When to use teams? Ask: Is work complex? Does it create a common purpose? Are members highly interdependent?

CH 11: COMMUNICATION

Process Model: Sender -> Encodes Message -> Channel -> Receiver Decodes -> Feedback loop. (Noise = barriers).

Channels: Formal: Established by org, transmits task info. Informal: Social/personal messages.

Direction & Networks

  • Downward: Assign goals, instructions. (1-way).
  • Upward: Feedback, report progress/problems.
  • Lateral: Between peers; saves time, coordination.

Formal Small Group Networks:

  • Chain: Rigidly follows chain of command.
  • Wheel: Central figure acts as conduit for all.
  • All-Channel: All members actively communicate (best for self-managed teams).

The Grapevine: Informal network. 75% accurate. Emerges when situations are important, ambiguous, or cause anxiety.

Channel Richness & Barriers

Media Richness: Ability to handle multiple cues simultaneously, facilitate rapid feedback, and be personal. High: Face-to-Face, Video. Low: Memos, Bulletins.

Barriers to Communication:

  • Filtering: Sender manipulates info so it seems more favorable.
  • Selective Perception: Receiver selectively hears based on needs/background.
  • Info Overload: Info exceeds processing capacity.
  • Emotions: Affects interpretation.
  • Language/Jargon: Words mean different things.
  • Silence: Ignoring issues.
  • Apprehension: Social anxiety regarding oral/written comms.

CH 12: LEADERSHIP

Lead vs Mgmt: Leaders challenge status quo, create vision, inspire. Managers formulate detailed plans, oversee operations, maintain order.

Trait & Behavioral Theories

Trait (Leaders are born): Focus on personal qualities. Big Five framework: Extraversion is the strongest predictor. Conscientiousness & Openness also linked. Emotional Intelligence (EI) is crucial.

Behavioral (Leaders can be trained):

  • Ohio State: Initiating Structure (defining roles/goals) vs Consideration (trust/respect for feelings).
  • Michigan: Production-Oriented (task focus) vs Employee-Oriented (interpersonal focus).
  • Managerial Grid (Blake/Mouton): 9x9 matrix. (9,9) "Team Management" is ideal.

Contingency Theories

Fiedler Model: Effective performance depends on match between leader style & situation. Assumption: Leadership style is FIXED. Evaluated via LPC (Least Preferred Coworker) questionnaire. High LPC = Relationship oriented. Low LPC = Task oriented.
Situational Control: 1) Leader-member relations, 2) Task structure, 3) Position power. Task-oriented best in very favorable/unfavorable situations. Relation-oriented best in moderate.

Cognitive Resource Theory: Extension of Fiedler. Stress impacts leadership. Low stress -> Intelligence > Experience. High stress -> Experience > Intelligence.

Path-Goal Theory (House): Leader's job is to assist followers in attaining goals & provide direction/support. Styles are flexible:

  • Directive: Gives specific guidance (best for ambiguous tasks).
  • Supportive: Friendly, shows concern (best for stressful tasks).
  • Participative: Consults with followers.
  • Achievement: Sets high goals, expects high performance.

LMX (Leader-Member Exchange): Leaders create "In-Groups" (trusted, get disproportionate attention/rewards, lower turnover) and "Out-Groups" based on time pressure & compatibility.

Charismatic & Full Range

Charismatic: Have vision, take personal risks, sensitive to follower needs, exhibit unconventional behaviors.
Becoming one: Maintain optimistic view, use passion as catalyst, tap into emotions.
Dark Side: Personal goals override org goals; narcissism.

Full Range Leadership Model:

Transactional (Management):

  • Laissez-Faire: Abdicates responsibilities.
  • MBE-Passive: Intervenes only if standards aren't met.
  • MBE-Active: Watches/searches for deviations.
  • Contingent Reward: Contracts exchange of rewards for effort.

Transformational (Inspires):

  • Idealized Influence: Provides vision, gains respect/trust.
  • Inspirational Motivation: Communicates high expectations.
  • Intellectual Stimulation: Promotes intelligence/problem solving.
  • Individualized Consideration: Gives personal attention, coaches.

CH 14: CONFLICT & NEG.

Conflict: Process that begins when one party perceives another has negatively affected something they care about.

Transitions in Thought:

  • Traditional: All conflict is harmful/bad. Must be avoided.
  • Interactionist: Conflict is a positive force and absolutely necessary for effective performance.
  • Resolution-Focused: Conflict is inevitable; focus on productive conflict resolution.

Types & Styles of Conflict

  • Task: Relates to content/goals. (Functional if low/mod).
  • Relationship: Interpersonal. (Always Dysfunctional).
  • Process: How work gets done. (Functional if low).

Conflict Handling Intentions (Styles):

  • Competing: Assertive & Uncooperative (Win-Lose).
  • Collaborating: Assertive & Cooperative (Win-Win).
  • Avoiding: Unassertive & Uncooperative (Lose-Lose).
  • Accommodating: Unassertive & Cooperative (Lose-Win).
  • Compromising: Mid-range on both (Partial Win/Lose).

Perceptual Distortions: Errors in judging conflict (e.g., assuming a fixed pie, anchoring on initial offers).

Negotiation

Process where 2+ parties exchange goods/services and attempt to agree upon the exchange rate.

Distributive Bargaining: Focus on claiming value. Fixed pie, Win-Lose, opposed interests, low info sharing, short-term focus.

Integrative Bargaining: Focus on creating value. Expand the pie, Win-Win, congruent interests, high info sharing, long-term focus.

Key Negotiation Terms

  • BATNA: Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement. This determines your lowest acceptable value. It is your ultimate source of power.
  • Target Point: What you ideally want to achieve.
  • Reservation (Resistance) Point: The lowest acceptable outcome (Bottom line). Walking away point.
  • Bargaining Zone (ZOPA): The space between the buyer's reservation point and the seller's reservation point. Overlap means agreement is possible.

Strategies for Creating Value

  • Assume value CAN be created.
  • Take a cooperative approach; build trust.
  • Logrolling: Trade off issues that are most important to you for what is most important to them.
  • Make package deals, not single-issue offers.
  • Make multiple offers simultaneously.

RAPID RECALL & MATRICES

The Managerial Grid (Blake/Mouton)

  • (9,9) Team Mgmt: High people, High prod.
  • (1,9) Country Club: High people, Low prod.
  • (9,1) Authority-Comp: Low people, High prod.
  • (1,1) Impoverished: Low people, Low prod.
  • (5,5) Middle of Road: Moderate on both.

When to use Conflict Styles

  • Competing: Emergencies, unpopular actions.
  • Collaborating: Merging different perspectives, integrative solutions.
  • Avoiding: Trivial issues, need cooling off.
  • Accommodating: When you're wrong, building social credits for later.
  • Compromising: Mutually exclusive goals, need a temporary fix.

Performance Formulas

  • Actual Perf = Potential Perf - Process Losses.
  • Synergy: 2 + 2 = 5. (Whole > Sum of parts).
  • Process Loss: 2 + 2 = 3. (Coordination issues).
